Activities

Antique Shopping

Snohomish is known for its historical downtown and is often referred to as the antique capital of the Northwest. Here you will find multiple antique shops filled with hundreds of rare and ancient relics. The antique shops can be found in downtown Snohomish on both first and second street. Second street actually has an antique mall. Pro-tip: parking can be busy so make sure to show up early in order to get a good spot.

Centennial Trail

The Centennial Trail has over 30 miles of paved trail from Snohomish to Skagit County. The trail is quite roomy averaging about 10 feet wide. This multipurpose trail is good for walking, running, biking, rollerblading, skate boarding, and riding horses. There is even a equestrian dirt trail that runs parallel with most of the trail. There is plenty of picnic tables and benches along the way to take a stop and enjoy a scenic view. There is also shelters and bathrooms spaced out periodically along the trail. The trail currently connects Snohomish, Lake Stevens and Arlington and many points in between. Make sure to follow proper trail etiquette, and note that there has been bear and cougar sighting so make sure to be safe.

Lord Hill Regional Park

The Lord Hill regional park is ranked as one of the best places to go trail running in Washington and offers miles of scenic and forestrytrails. The trail is open only during the day, and is good for walking, hiking, trail running, mountain biking and riding horses. There is a designated parking lot for horse trailers. The park has many ponds and lakes as well as excellent view points. There is plenty of wildlife to be seen. There is a large population of birds, animals and fish. The most common animal found here is the beaver. In addition to this the area is also inhabited by mice, skunks, deer and the occasional bear and bobcat. As always, you should exercise proper trail etiquette and practice the leave no trace model while using the trail.

Skydive Snohomish

Snohomish has its own Airport, and it is ran by Snohomish Flying Services. In additon to offering flights in Airplanes and Helicopters, they also offer Skydiving. The Travel Channel states:

"Snohomish is rated as one of the World's Best Places to go Skydiving..."

If you like adventure and consider yourself an adrenaline junky you can sign up for your first skydive. The Tandem Dive will set you back just over $200.
